Energy consumption in data processing

The machine learning community is increasingly aware of the environmental impact of data processing, especially since the emergence of deep learning models. The increase power consumption to train and deploy deep leanring models has set the machine learning community to consider efficiency as a key aspect of their designs. However, recent studies [1, 2, 3] and regulations [4] tend to shift the focus towards a different concept: frugality.

Frugality over efficiency

As most of the literature focuses on optimizing for efficiency, this dashboard encapsulates the concept of frugality. Efficiency aims for the maximization of the performance to energy ratio, with little to no consideration of the context of use. Frugality, on the other hand, is context specific and aims to optimize the performance and energy consumption based on real-life interpretable rules. As such, frugality questions the objectives of an application, and can lead to more sustainable, realistic, and user-centric designs.

Fuzzy-Based Frugality Score

This dashboard implements a Mamdani fuzzy inference system (FIS) [5] to compute a frugality score based on performance and energy consumption. Fuzzy logic is particularly suitable for multi-criteria decision-making tasks involving uncertainty and human subjectivity [6, 7]. As the performance and energy consumption of a system can be considered as human subjective concepts, a fuzzy-based approach permits to formalize the reasoning process of the user and to compute an interpretable frugality score.

Guide

Use the sidebar to input system parameters and toggle between tabs to explore the membership functions, rule base, and results.
